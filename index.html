💡 Japan Travel Tips

  • 🚅 JR Pass: Consider getting a JR Pass if you'll be taking multiple Shinkansen rides. Can be cost-effective for long-distance travel.
  • 🚇 IC Cards (Suica/Pasmo): Get an IC card at any station — works on trains, subways, buses, and even convenience stores. Just tap and go!
  • 📱 Apps: Download Google Maps (works great in Japan), Hyperdia for train schedules, Google Translate (with camera), and Navitime for walking directions.
  • 💰 Cash is King: While cards are widely accepted in cities, many smaller shops and rural areas are cash-only. Always carry yen.
  • 🔌 Plugs: Japan uses Type A/B plugs (same as Thailand). No adapter needed! Voltage is 100V (compatible with most devices).
  • 🌡️ November/December Weather: Cool and crisp, 5-15°C in Tokyo/Osaka/Kyoto. Fuji area can be near freezing. Bring layers, a warm jacket, gloves, and a scarf.
  • 🗻 Mt. Fuji Viewing: Best views are on clear mornings from Lake Kawaguchiko. Chureito Pagoda at sunrise is iconic but requires climbing 398 steps!
  • 🏯 Temple Etiquette: Remove shoes before entering temple buildings. Dress modestly. Be quiet and respectful. Don't take photos where prohibited.
  • 👘 Kimono Rental: Reserve in advance, especially on weekends. Plan 2-3 hours for rental and photos. Returns are usually by 5-6 PM.
  • 🏰 Tokyo Disneyland: Arrive at opening. Use the Tokyo Disney Resort app for wait times and mobile ordering. FastPass is now paid (Disney Premier Access).
  • 🍜 Food: Try ramen, sushi, tempura, yakitori, okonomiyaki (Osaka), takoyaki (Osaka), and regional specialties. Convenience stores (konbini) are excellent!
  • 🚶 Walking: Japan is a walking country. Wear comfortable shoes. You'll easily walk 15,000+ steps per day.
  • 🗑️ No Trash Bins: Public trash cans are rare. Carry a small bag for your trash and dispose of it at your hotel or convenience store.
  • 🚭 Smoking Areas: Smoking is banned in most public areas. Look for designated smoking zones. Don't smoke while walking!
  • 🚕 Taxis: Expensive. Use trains and subways — they're faster, cheaper, and more reliable. Left rear door opens automatically.
  • 📶 Wi-Fi/SIM: Get a pocket Wi-Fi or eSIM at the airport. Free Wi-Fi is available at stations, cafes, and convenience stores but can be spotty.
  • 🛍️ Shopping: Tax-free shopping for foreigners (minimum ¥5,000). Bring your passport. Don't miss Don Quijote for everything!
  • 🎌 Bowing: A slight bow is polite when greeting or thanking someone. Not required from tourists but appreciated.
  • 🚃 Train Etiquette: Quiet on trains. No phone calls. Keep conversations low priority. Priority seats for elderly/pregnant/disabled.
  • 🎫 Reservations: Book popular restaurants, kimono rentals, and some attractions in advance, especially on weekends.
